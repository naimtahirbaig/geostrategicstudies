About the Organisation

Our Mission & Mandate

The Geo Strategic Studies Organisation (GSSO) produces original research that bridges the gap between academic rigour and policy relevance — making complex geopolitical realities accessible to scholars, diplomats, policymakers, and the informed public.

01

Independent Research

We produce peer-reviewed and independently published research across international relations, nuclear strategy, regional security, and emerging threats — free from institutional or governmental bias.

02

Policy Analysis

Our strategic briefs and analyses inform decision-makers navigating great-power competition, alliance dynamics, arms control, and regional instability — from the Strait of Hormuz to the Indo-Pacific.

03

Online Research Journal

The GSSO Journal of International Security Studies publishes original research papers, book reviews, and policy commentaries. We welcome submissions from scholars worldwide across our core domains.

About

Dr. Naim Tahir Baig is a globally recognised international relations scholar, strategic analyst, and one of the most prolific authors in the field of geopolitical scholarship. As founding CEO of the Geo Strategic Studies Organisation, he leads its mission to produce rigorous, policy-relevant research that bridges academic scholarship with accessible public discourse.

His work spans nuclear deterrence, great-power competition, regional security architectures, Islamic political thought, and the intersection of technology with statecraft — with particular depth on South Asia, the Middle East, and U.S.-China strategic rivalry.

For Authors

Submission Guidelines

The GSSO Journal welcomes original research from scholars, analysts, and practitioners worldwide. We publish peer-reviewed papers, policy briefs, strategic commentaries, and book reviews.

Scope & Topics

We accept submissions within these core domains:

  • International Relations (theory and practice)
  • Nuclear Strategy, Deterrence, and Arms Control
  • Geopolitics and Grand Strategy
  • Security Studies and Defence Analysis
  • Political Science and Comparative Governance
  • Peace, Conflict Resolution, and Post-Conflict Studies
  • Technology, AI, and Strategic Competition
  • Regional Studies (South Asia, Middle East, Indo-Pacific)
  • Maritime Security and International Law

Manuscript Requirements

Research papers should be 5,000–12,000 words including references. Policy briefs should be 2,000–4,000 words. All submissions must include an abstract (200–300 words), keywords, and a complete bibliography. Papers must be original and not under consideration elsewhere.

Review Process

All submissions undergo double-blind peer review. Initial editorial screening takes 7–10 days, with full peer review typically completed within 6–8 weeks. Authors receive structured reviewer feedback with revision guidance.

Citation Style

The journal follows the Chicago Manual of Style (Author-Date system). Footnotes may be used for substantive notes. All references must be included in a consolidated bibliography.
